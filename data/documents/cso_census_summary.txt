Census of Population 2022 — Summary Results for Community Intelligence

Population Overview:
- Ireland's population reached 5.15 million in Census 2022, up 8% from 2016.
- Dublin remains the most populated county with 1.46 million residents.
- The fastest-growing counties were Meath (+12.6%), Kildare (+11.6%), and Longford (+10.9%).
- Rural depopulation continues in parts of the West and Northwest, particularly Leitrim and Roscommon.

Employment & Income:
- National employment rate: 69.2% of working-age population (15-64).
- Dublin has the highest employment rate at 76.1%, followed by Kildare (74.2%) and Wicklow (73.4%).
- Lowest employment rates: Donegal (60.3%), Leitrim (60.8%), and Longford (61.2%).
- Average annual household income: €55,000 in Dublin, €31,000 in Leitrim.
- The tech sector accounts for 12% of Dublin employment, concentrated in the Docklands and Sandyford.

Commuting Patterns:
- 1.2 million workers commute daily, with average commute time of 28.2 minutes nationally.
- Dublin commuters face the longest average commute: 34 minutes.
- 68% of commuters drive alone; only 12% use public transport nationally.
- Remote working: 32% of workers reported working from home at least one day per week, up from 7% in 2016.
- Counties with highest remote work rates: Leitrim (41%), Roscommon (38%), Galway (36%).

Housing Tenure:
- 66% of households are owner-occupied (down from 70% in 2016).
- 18% are private renters (up from 15% in 2016).
- 9% are in social housing.
- Average household size: 2.75 persons nationally.
- Dublin has the highest proportion of renters at 29%.

Age Demographics:
- Median age: 38.8 years nationally.
- Youngest county: Meath (median 35.2 years).
- Oldest county: Roscommon (median 42.1 years).
- 28% of the population is under 18; 15% is over 65.

Education:
- 45% of adults (25-64) hold a third-level qualification, highest in the EU.
- Dublin (52%), Galway (48%), and Kildare (47%) have the most educated populations.
- Counties with lowest third-level attainment: Donegal (32%), Cavan (33%), Longford (34%).
