Daft.ie Irish Rental Market Report Q4 2024

Market Overview:
Daft.ie is Ireland's largest property listings platform, with over 95% market coverage for rental and sale listings. This report analyses Q4 2024 rental market trends.

National Rental Market:
- Average asking rent nationally: €1,875 per month (up 4.2% YoY).
- Total rental listings on November 1st: 3,800 — up from a record low of 1,100 in 2022 but still 65% below the 2019 average of 11,000.
- Average time-to-let: 14 days nationally, 7 days in Dublin.
- Rental demand outstrips supply by an estimated 3:1 ratio nationally, 5:1 in Dublin.

Dublin Market:
- Average asking rent: €2,350/month.
- 1-bed apartment: €1,750/month. 2-bed apartment: €2,200/month. 3-bed house: €2,800/month.
- Approximately 1,200 rental listings available at any time.
- Highest rents: Dublin 4 (Ballsbridge/Donnybrook) at €3,200/month average.
- Most affordable Dublin areas: Dublin 15 (Blanchardstown) at €1,800/month, Dublin 24 (Tallaght) at €1,750/month.

Regional Markets:
- Cork City: €1,650/month average. Strong demand from pharma/tech workers.
- Galway City: €1,550/month. Severe shortage — only 80-100 listings at any time.
- Limerick City: €1,350/month. Growing tech hub (Analog Devices, Johnson & Johnson).
- Waterford City: €1,150/month. Most affordable city, attracting remote workers.

Commuter Belt Trends:
- Kildare: €1,450/month (+8% YoY). Maynooth and Celbridge seeing strongest growth.
- Meath: €1,400/month (+7% YoY). Ashbourne and Ratoath popular with Dublin commuters.
- Wicklow: €1,500/month (+6% YoY). Bray and Greystones command premium rents.
- Louth: €1,300/month (+9% YoY). Drogheda emerging as affordable Dublin alternative.

Affordable Alternatives:
- For renters priced out of Dublin, the report identifies these "value" counties:
  - Leitrim: €728/month — Ireland's most affordable county. Ideal for remote workers.
  - Longford: €762/month — Good value, improving broadband connectivity.
  - Roscommon: €745/month — Low rents, low congestion, but limited employment.
  - Offaly: €820/month — Central location, good motorway access to Dublin.
  - Laois: €865/month — Portlaoise has direct rail to Dublin (55 minutes).

Property Type Breakdown (National):
- Apartments: 42% of rental listings (predominantly Dublin and cities).
- Houses: 48% of rental listings.
- Studios: 5% of listings.
- Shared accommodation: 5% of listings.

Investor Sentiment:
- Buy-to-let investment declining: 15% of landlords plan to sell within 2 years.
- Institutional investors now account for 25% of new rental supply (up from 5% in 2018).
- REITs and institutional landlords concentrated in Dublin and Cork.
- Small landlords exiting due to tax burden, RPZ restrictions, and regulatory complexity.

Forecast:
- Rents expected to grow 3-5% in 2025, moderating from 2024 levels.
- Supply expected to improve marginally as new-build completions increase.
- Cost rental and social housing will absorb some demand pressure.
- Remote work continues to support demand in rural and regional areas.
