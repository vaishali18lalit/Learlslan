TII National Roads & Transport Infrastructure Report 2024

Traffic Volume Overview:
- Ireland's national road network carries 38 billion vehicle-kilometres annually.
- The M50 (Dublin orbital motorway) is the busiest road, with 140,000+ vehicles per day.
- Traffic volumes have recovered to 104% of pre-COVID levels nationally.
- Dublin accounts for 35% of all national traffic despite having 29% of the population.

Congestion Analysis by County:
- Dublin: average peak-hour delay of 28 minutes. Worst corridors: M50 southbound, N7 Naas Road, N11 Stillorgan.
- Cork: 18 minutes average delay. Jack Lynch Tunnel and Dunkettle Interchange are primary bottlenecks.
- Galway: 15 minutes. The absence of a city ring road forces all traffic through the city centre.
- Limerick: 14 minutes. The Limerick Tunnel has reduced congestion significantly since opening.
- Rural counties (Leitrim, Roscommon, Longford): 4-6 minutes average delay. Minimal congestion.

Major Infrastructure Projects:
- DART+ Programme: Expanding Dublin's electrified rail network from 50km to 150km. Serving Drogheda, Maynooth, Celbridge, and Bray. Completion: 2028.
- MetroLink: 19km underground metro from Swords to Charlemont (Dublin). Completion: 2035.
- BusConnects Dublin: Complete redesign of Dublin's bus network with 230km of dedicated bus corridors. Rollout: 2024-2028.
- Galway City Ring Road (N6): 18km orbital route to relieve city centre congestion. Under planning.
- Cork Metropolitan Area Transport Strategy (CMATS): Light rail, BRT, and cycling infrastructure. 2024-2040.
- M20 Cork-Limerick Motorway: Direct motorway link between Ireland's 2nd and 3rd cities. Under planning.

Public Transport Usage:
- Dublin: 45% of city-centre workers use public transport (bus, rail, Luas).
- Cork: 12% public transport mode share.
- Galway: 8% public transport mode share.
- Rural Ireland: <3% public transport usage. Car dependency is near-total.

Commuting Cost Estimates:
- Average Irish commuter spends €4,500-6,000 per year on transport (fuel, insurance, tolls, parking).
- Dublin commuters face the highest costs: €6,200/year average.
- Remote workers save an estimated €3,500/year in commuting costs.
- Each minute of congestion delay costs approximately €0.50 in lost productivity and fuel.

Road Safety:
- 150 road fatalities in 2024, down from 166 in 2023.
- Rural roads account for 80% of fatalities despite carrying 40% of traffic.
- Speed limits on rural national roads reduced from 100km/h to 80km/h in high-risk zones.

Electric Vehicle Infrastructure:
- 4,500 public EV charge points nationally (target: 12,000 by 2030).
- Dublin has the densest network; rural coverage remains sparse.
- EV registration share: 22% of new car sales in 2024.
