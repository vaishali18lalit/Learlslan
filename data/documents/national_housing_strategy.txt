National Development Plan 2021-2030 - Housing Strategy Summary

Context:
The Irish Government has pledged €4 billion annually to increase the supply of affordable housing across key counties. The focus is on dense urban centers and strategic commuting towns.

Specific County Highlights:
- Dublin: Massive investment in high-density apartments and the expansion of the DART+ public transport network. Expected to increase supply but rents will likely remain high due to persistent demand.
- Cork: The "Cork 2040" initiative aims to double the population by investing heavily in the Docklands revitalization. Rents are expected to grow moderately.
- Limerick: Strong investment in FDI (Foreign Direct Investment) parks. Recommended for young professionals seeking tech jobs outside Dublin.
- Galway: Focus on student housing and suburban development. Traffic alleviation via the anticipated Galway City Ring Road.
- Midlands (Laois, Offaly, Westmeath): Highlighted as primary targets for the "Remote Work Strategy". State-sponsored remote working hubs are being deployed in these areas to attract remote workers seeking lower costs of living.

Incentives:
First-time buyers utilizing the "Help to Buy" scheme can receive up to €30,000 towards the deposit of a newly built home. Also, the "Croí Cónaithe" scheme offers up to €50,000 to refurbish vacant properties in rural towns and villages.
