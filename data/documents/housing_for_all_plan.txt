Housing for All — A New Housing Plan for Ireland (2021-2030)

Executive Summary:
Housing for All is the Irish Government's flagship housing strategy, committing €4 billion annually to address the housing crisis through four pathways: supporting home ownership, increasing rental supply, addressing homelessness, and planning reform.

Key Targets:
- Deliver 300,000 new homes by 2030 (average 33,000 per year).
- 90,000 social homes (local authority and approved housing bodies).
- 36,000 affordable purchase homes.
- 18,000 cost rental homes (rents set at 25% below market rate).
- 156,000 private market homes.

Pathway 1 — Supporting Home Ownership:
- Help to Buy Scheme: First-time buyers can claim a tax refund of up to €30,000 (10% of purchase price, max €500,000) for newly built homes.
- First Home Scheme: Shared equity scheme where the State takes up to 30% equity stake, reducing the mortgage needed.
- Local Authority Affordable Purchase Scheme: Homes sold at below-market prices with a clawback on resale.
- Affordable housing targets focus on Dublin, Cork, Galway, and commuter counties (Kildare, Meath, Wicklow).

Pathway 2 — Addressing Rental Supply:
- Rent Pressure Zones (RPZs): Annual rent increases capped at 2% in designated areas.
- Cost Rental: State-subsidised rental targeting middle-income workers. Rents typically €1,000-1,200/month in Dublin.
- Tax incentives for institutional landlords to build purpose-built rental accommodation.
- Pre-letting planning permission to accelerate build-to-rent developments.

Pathway 3 — Addressing Homelessness:
- Target: End long-term homelessness by 2030.
- Housing First approach: Provide permanent housing with wraparound supports.
- 10,000 additional social housing units per year.
- Emergency accommodation to be phased out in favour of permanent solutions.

Pathway 4 — Planning Reform:
- Large-scale Residential Development (LRD) fast-track planning for 100+ unit schemes.
- Residential Zoned Land Tax (RZLT): 3% annual tax on zoned, serviced land that remains undeveloped.
- Streamlined planning for modular and off-site construction methods.
- Updated building regulations for energy efficiency (NZEB standard).

Regional Focus:
- Dublin: High-density apartment development in the Docklands, Poolbeg, and Clongriffin SDZs.
- Cork: Cork Docklands regeneration — 25,000 new homes planned.
- Limerick: Colbert Quarter and Opera Site regeneration.
- Galway: Ardaun and Murrough SDZ developments.
- Midlands & Rural: Croí Cónaithe scheme for town centre revitalisation.

Funding:
- Total investment: €4 billion per year (2021-2030).
- Land Development Agency (LDA) capitalised with €1.25 billion to develop State-owned land.
- Housing Finance Agency provides low-cost loans to approved housing bodies.
- Exchequer funding for social housing increased 40% since 2020.
