RTB Rent Index Report Q4 2024 — Key Findings

Overview:
The Residential Tenancies Board (RTB) Rent Index tracks standardised average rents across Ireland using data from all registered tenancies. This report covers Q4 2024.

National Trends:
- National standardised average rent reached €1,612 per month in Q4 2024, up 5.6% year-on-year.
- Dublin remains the most expensive county with average rents of €2,102 per month.
- Outside Dublin, average rents are €1,334 per month.
- Rent inflation has moderated from the 2022 peak of 12.6% to 5.6% in 2024.

County-Level Highlights:
- Dublin: €2,102/month (+4.8% YoY). Highest rents in Pembroke, Rathmines, and Ballsbridge EDs.
- Cork: €1,489/month (+6.2% YoY). Cork City rents growing faster than county average.
- Galway: €1,378/month (+7.1% YoY). Galway City experiencing acute supply shortage.
- Limerick: €1,245/month (+5.4% YoY). Castletroy and Raheen seeing strong demand from UL students and tech workers.
- Waterford: €1,089/month (+4.1% YoY). Most affordable of the cities.

Affordability Analysis:
- Nationally, 42% of tenants spend more than 30% of net income on rent (housing-cost-burdened).
- In Dublin, this figure rises to 58%.
- The most affordable counties for renters are Leitrim (€728/month), Roscommon (€745/month), and Longford (€762/month).
- Rent Pressure Zones (RPZs) cap annual rent increases at 2% in designated areas, covering most urban centres.

Supply Indicators:
- Total registered tenancies: 342,000 nationally.
- New tenancy registrations fell 8% YoY, indicating reduced turnover and fewer available units.
- Dublin has approximately 1,200 rental listings at any given time, down from 4,500 in 2019.

Rental Yield Analysis:
- National average gross rental yield: 5.2%.
- Dublin yields are compressed at 3.8% due to high property prices.
- Highest yields found in Longford (6.1%), Leitrim (5.9%), and Roscommon (5.8%).
- Yields above 5% are considered attractive for buy-to-let investors.

Policy Context:
- The Residential Tenancies (Amendment) Act 2024 extended RPZ protections.
- The Housing Assistance Payment (HAP) scheme supports 62,000 households nationally.
- The Cost Rental model (targeting rents 25% below market) has delivered 1,200 units to date.
