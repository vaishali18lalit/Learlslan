Croí Cónaithe Towns Fund & Vacant Property Refurbishment Grant

Overview:
The Croí Cónaithe scheme is a Government of Ireland initiative to bring vacant and derelict properties back into use, particularly in rural towns and villages. It offers substantial grants to individuals who purchase and refurbish vacant or derelict properties for use as their primary residence.

Vacant Property Refurbishment Grant:
- Grant of up to €50,000 for refurbishing a vacant property.
- Additional top-up of €20,000 (total €70,000) if the property is confirmed derelict.
- Property must have been vacant for at least 2 years.
- Must be used as the applicant's primary residence for at least 5 years.
- Available in all counties, but primarily targets towns with populations under 10,000.

Eligibility:
- Open to Irish citizens and residents.
- No income threshold — available to all.
- No build-date restriction (originally limited to pre-2007, restriction removed in 2023 update).
- Works must bring the property to a minimum BER of B2.
- Applicant must not own another residential property (exceptions for inherited properties).

How It Works:
1. Identify a vacant/derelict property in an eligible town or village.
2. Apply to the local authority with a cost estimate and BER assessment.
3. Receive approval and commence works.
4. Grant paid in stages: 50% at commencement, 50% on completion and BER certification.

Impact to Date:
- Over 8,000 applications received since launch in 2022.
- 3,200 grants approved, with 1,800 properties completed and occupied.
- Average grant amount: €42,000.
- Highest uptake in Cork (520 applications), Galway (410), Kerry (380), and Tipperary (350).
- Lowest uptake in Dublin (45 applications) — fewer vacant properties in urban areas.

Target Areas:
- The scheme is particularly impactful in the Midlands (Laois, Offaly, Westmeath, Longford) where vacancy rates exceed 15%.
- Western counties (Mayo, Roscommon, Leitrim, Sligo) have high dereliction rates and benefit significantly.
- Towns like Boyle (Roscommon), Ballinasloe (Galway), and Thurles (Tipperary) have seen notable revitalisation.

Related Schemes:
- Ready to Build Scheme: Serviced sites in towns provided at reduced cost for self-builders.
- Town Centre First Policy: Prioritises development in existing town centres over greenfield sites.
- Rural Regeneration and Development Fund: €1 billion for rural town infrastructure.
- SEAI retrofit grants can be combined with Croí Cónaithe for energy upgrades.

Financial Comparison:
- Average cost to refurbish a vacant 3-bed house: €80,000-120,000.
- With Croí Cónaithe grant (€50,000-70,000), net cost: €30,000-50,000.
- Comparable new-build 3-bed in the same area: €250,000-350,000.
- Monthly mortgage on refurbished property: approximately €400-600 vs €1,200-1,600 for new-build.
